*Wooden planks holding bats made of its own flesh, bomber planes with alligator skull fronts, sharks that grew legs just to wear Nike.*

*We used to laugh at [AI brain-rot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI_slop) because of its mild disconnect from reality, yet being legible enough to mimic something real. It resided in the uncanny valley of our comprehension of the world and how it mixed. It used to be easy to dismiss.*

*But the Eigenslop is different.*

*The attention economy has managed to [mathematically derive the average of human desire](https://youtu.be/PFDu9oVAE-g?si=K_mTLNS29SEdh-zB&t=81). Distilled into a weaponised stream of pixels and frequencies that knows exactly what triggers any intended emotional response by exploiting known neural reward pathways. It does not persuade you. It does not argue with you. Through rewiring the reward pathway in your brain, bypassing language entirely, it will leave you feeling exactly what it wants you to feel, in the shortest time it needs.*

This is a time that has not yet fully arrived, so it is preventable. I will be outlining the inflection points, that need to be controlled, so we never see this become a high likelihood future.

Every major leap in [persuasion tech](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persuasive_technology) has been flagged as mind control by the general public until they learned to see the strings. Persuasion is achieved through a higher [asymmetrical advantage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_asymmetry) against the receiver of the information. Through controlling the [growth in worldview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conceptual_model) of the receiver, any form of consent they provide can be manufactured in the adversaries favour.

This is NOT consent. Consent requires two parties with the same information and sound judgement to be able to make sound judgement on an action. The adversary is [manufacturing the consent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manufacturing_Consent) of the receiver in this situation. Institutional bodies and power concentrations (i.e. governing bodies, monopolies & oligopolies, parents) are amazing at this usually, since they are the ones who will decide what information forms the worldview of the receiver to the point where any potential break from the power structure requires terminology outside of their conception. Resulting in the receiver fighting a battle on flawed foundations, or being dismissed as incoherent if any arguments are even held.

**Once you can control a significant proportion of the mediums of information uptake, you can mould the prevailing narrative to whatever you choose. This is called shifting the [Overton window](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overton_window).**

This already exists and is largely implemented against forms of communal action, bot farms get deployed to sow alternate disorientating narratives to cause arguments and destabilise the discourse surrounding the core goals. They implement these in social media comment sections where the top relevant comments will be selected based on what will retain you, whether that be inflammatory or in-grouping. They do this in websites UX where they add testimonials to give you the feeling that someone else has vetted the service so that you are more enticed to purchase.

The printing press, the radio jingle, the infinite scroll; each time a new medium will dirty the cognitive hygiene of the people, and the major powers will have a technological arms race to be able to optimise the medium to be able to shift its goals in their favour.

Eigenslop is just another form of raising the tensile strength of the strings that can pull us. I don’t trust that the companies, who will be the first movers, will have our best interests in mind.

It is a foundational principle in cognitive science that the human brain evolved to process information primarily as narratives than isolated data points. Our brains seek these stories out everywhere and they reward us when we find them. Decades ago, writer Joseph Campbell formalised this millennia old process into what we call the [hero’s journey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hero%27s_journey). He theorised that there are precise beats required to trigger a complete emotional experience, and if you hit these beats in the right sequence, it can produce predictable emotional responses in the audience. Many more frameworks followed, but they all boil down to the same underlying insight. The issue is that these can be mathematically formalised, and engineered to optimally produce the beats in shortening lengths of time.

**By compressing the throughline and collapsing the time between subversions and progressions, the payoff approaches instantaneous.**

New short form media across social media platforms have created a whole field out of this, this is the field I grew up in and was a frontrunner. Creators share methods of hitting the narrative beats across text, video and audio simultaneously to be able to maximise watch time over video length. We prioritise metrics: views-to-likes, views-to-followers, followers-to-likes, comments-to-shares, likes-to-shares. Many more permutations to create new metrics from pre existing metrics as a science to detract more information outside the standard demographic ones. This control of measurement will start to explode out over time. In the step above the creator space we have the [social listening](https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/marketing/social-listening) space for brands, their technology as of today has more than 300 metrics on some of the software I personally have used. These can aggregate across almost all mediums of the internet and nothing constricts the output methods.

**The good part is that like all information, [compression has its limits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shannon%27s_source_coding_theorem). A thirty second video can make you feel something, but it cannot make you feel everything. There are trade offs. Depth is sacrificed for speed.**

However, conceptually, Eigenslop eliminates the trade off entirely.

It does not need a story. It needs only the shape of a story. The mathematical fingerprint of an emotional journey, and it can replicate that fingerprint in pure sensory data. its output mediums only needs to be colours, frequency and timing. All optimised to hit your reward pathway while being finetuned against a model of you every recurring second you live. Everything can be stripped of its context, converted to its equivalent sensory trigger and deployed directly, distilled in its purest form.

As a verbatim statement, bypassing the legibility layer is dangerous. A story can be questioned because it has characters with associated logic and motivations that can be reinterpreted or rejected or discussed. This is not the case with Eigenslop. A frequency curve has no associated argument, we cant debate why it struck 183Hz instead of 174Hz without knowing the underlying architecture, and then its an asymmetrical power concentration issue again. We cant argue the ideology behind the use of blue instead of purple in the specific X,Y position of the mulch on the screen. The agenda is outsourced from the medium itself into the algorithm that shaped it.

The nudging architecture of malls already exists today. This was assembled over decades of iterative design. The mall you walk through is not neutral space. It is a carefully engineered environment, with its goal to keep you inside, disoriented, and spending. Their aim is to [convert a rewarding experience into repeat patronage, and repeat patronage into loyalty](https://youtu.be/HfAzylVaFg8?si=8MtvgbdtPec_fBo8). For a rewarding experience they ensure that the layout creates a flow for the customer that is frictionless and intuitive, this is by stocking items in specific layouts and sections that consumers who buy a certain product are likely to scan the shelves for the other complementary product enough to build a basket. They have specific standard layouts e.g.[ the racetrack layout](https://youtu.be/70NgdopgJJs?si=vlvLwrydkKa1rjGi) that blocks view of certain categories so that people can constrain their options enough to make a decision. [Atmospherics](https://youtu.be/6JIg52Z5uN4?si=bV7M0L0sTE3KChK6) are a large factor of keeping customers in the store longer, they rely on lighting, colour, music, scent and taste. all of these can be tailored, niched down into demographics today, and to individuals tomorrow, along the Eigenslop pathway. None of this requires new technology.

We have a principle in sales, the only time you are selling something, is when the card touches the reader. until then everything else is just warming up the deal. Warming up a deal is a [game theory problem](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/QxZs5Za4qXBegXCgu/introduction-to-game-theory-sequence-guide), this pushes into the negotiation field. In game theory, the biggest advantage you can have is more information as that gives you the asymmetrical power. Given our current trajectory, it is highly likely that the corporation will hold the power. This gives the corporation the ability to push the customer down their [preferred decision tree.](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/EhEZoTFzys9EDmEXn/backward-reasoning-over-decision-trees)

**Losing the battle to win the war is no longer an option when the adversary wins every battle. The long term is merely a series of short terms.**

### Inflection points

Now that you can see some angles of my concerns, I would like to bring attention to the aforementioned key inflection points to intervene at. Push these now and the trajectory can shift from these futures to come to fruition.

#### 1. The Technical:

Before Eigenslop can target you, it must collect data about you. Before it can collect data, it must have sensors. Before it can have sensors, it must have infrastructure. Each of these is an inflection point. If we can regulate the sensors, the cameras, the microphones, the gait-trackers, we can starve the algorithm of the data it needs to function.

#### 2. The Policy:

Before Eigenslop can be deployed, it must be legal. Before it is legal, it must be regulated (or not). This is the most powerful lever we have. A single piece of legislation can ban the collection of bio-metric data in public spaces. A single international treaty can prohibit the use of AI optimised, dynamically targeted audio-visual stimuli. These are extensions of existing frameworks like GDPR and adjacent data rights acts, or privacy and surveillance legislation.

#### 3. The Cognitive:

Before Eigenslop can control you, it must bypass your awareness. Before it can bypass your awareness, it must exploit your predictability. This is where individual agency matters. If you can make yourself unpredictable, if you can flood your mental model with noise, you can inject some level of noise on their digital twin model on you. This is the hardest of the three as it requires you to essentially split yourself into internal and external action, an then disrupt your internals while still maintaining the external action, this is tiring and will likely lead to some form of autoimmune disorder from sustained stress.

### Personal Countermeasures Now

#### 1. Visual:

Tinted lenses, ND filter glasses, and diffraction lenses can block or scramble the specific wavelengths used for targeted visual stimuli. Even simple coloured tinted lenses can throw off the colour based priming that Eigenslop would potentially rely on. By doing this, you see everything differently so it noises their intended affect

#### 2. Audio:

Noise cancelling earbuds, frequency injecting devices, and background noise generation can disrupt the audio frequencies used for neural targeting through sound. Earbuds that change frequencies sporadically can prevent a clean lock.

#### 3. Cognitive:

Doublethink. Deliberately holding contradictory thoughts can flood your mental model with noise, making it harder for the system to predict your responses. in urban situations, countermeasures like grounding exercises, randomisation of shopping habits, and deliberate unpredictability can break the feedback loops for Eigenslop algorithms

### Effective Solutions in short term

#### Start using Local/on-device tech that doesn’t share data externally:

You don’t need to wait for Eigenslop to be banned, because the actual mechanism it depends on, your behavioural model leaving your possession, is already optional in most of the places you’d think it isn’t. On device inference already exists in your phones keyboard, your cameras scene detection and small parts of your voice assistant. The infrastructure for “your data never leaves your hardware” has been built, it has just been built quietly and marketed weakly, because it serves you instead of the platform. Choosing local first software over cloud personalised software, where the option exists, is a vote that costs you nothing but a slightly less optimised experience. That slight loss of optimisation is the whole point that will save you from sacrificing agency in future.

#### Persuasion detection classifiers and ai debate style adversarial argument flagging:

This is the tier I would ask the technologists reading this to take personally. A top layer filter on the input is not the most promising defence. A system that can look at incoming media and tell you, before you’ve felt anything, that what you’re looking at was tailored to make you feel something rather than to tell you something true, is. This is a live research agenda at the moment, still yet to be a product but i have seen [Tom Davidson propose a methodology for training systems to recognise persuasive-regardless-of-truth arguments](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/zxmzBTwKkPMxQQcfR/let-s-use-ai-to-harden-human-defenses-against-ai) that should be implemented into your own conduct. Nobody has shipped the personal epistemic immune system yet. Somebody should. A blunt classifier should be enough. Im toying with some theoretical models at the moment, but the goal is to change the interaction from blind absorption to informed consent. This is technically viable with todays technology to get something crude going.

#### Policy:

Our current focus should be naming the gap before the law has to improvise an answer to it later, we cant trust that the law wont come up with some patchwork solution 5 years after the problem already ran its course. this field is in the midsection of cybersecurity, information security and marketing, but none of them protects attention, trust, or memory directly which is where Eigenslop resides.

The argument worth pushing into every relevant policy conversation today, not after the mall scenario is real, is that this midsection needs its own category, its own consent standard, its own disclosure requirement, separate from how we currently think about privacy or advertising. Biometric and physiological inference in public space should require the same explicit, specific consent that identity data requires, not be smuggled in under “improving your experience.”
